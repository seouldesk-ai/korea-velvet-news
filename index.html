Seoul's Jegi-dong Station Leads City for Senior Visitor Concentration

12 May 2026 · chosun.com

Seoul's Jegi-dong station has the highest proportion of senior visitors of any station in the city, owing to its proximity to the Seoul Yangnyeong herbal medicine material market and Gyeongdong Market — both accessible from exit two — as well as a concentration of affordable restaurants, barbers, and dance halls catering to older patrons. The area serves not only as a shopping destination but as a social hub where seniors meet friends and spend time. Korean Association of Oriental Medicine calls for Korean medicine to become national strategic industry

6 May 2026 · yonhapnewstv.co.kr

The Korean Association of Oriental Medicine issued a statement on 6 July calling for Korean traditional medicine to be designated a national strategic industry, citing rapid growth in foreign demand. Statistics from the Ministry of Health and Welfare show that 37,087 foreign nationals visited Korean medicine clinics in 2025, a 9.4% increase on the 34,535 recorded in 2024, while total foreign patients in South Korea exceeded two million for the first time, reaching 2,011,822. The association, which represents Korean medicine doctors across the country, attributed part of this surge to the global popularity of the 2025 drama 'Kdeheon' [GLOSSARY GAP], in which the lead character visits a Korean medicine clinic to receive herbal treatment, with the Seoul Korean Medicine Promotion Centre — believed to be the real-world model for the clinic depicted — recording a 50.9% month-on-month rise in foreign visitors to 1,720 in June 2025. The K-MEX 2026 exhibition, held at COEX in Seoul on 25 and 26 June, also showcased herbal medicine material, medical devices, and clinical systems related to Korean and integrative medicine, reflecting the sector's growing international profile. Despite the strong upward trend in demand, the association warned that supporting regulation and government investment remain wholly inadequate and that various regulatory barriers continue to impede growth. Industry voices are calling for Korean traditional medicine to be developed as a medical tourism brand, underpinned by an integrated industrial ecosystem covering licensing, export, and market-entry support. The Korea International Trade Association has projected the global traditional medicine market will reach 1,125 trillion won by 2027. The association urged the government to formally designate Korean traditional medicine as a national medical tourism brand, link it with K-beauty and K-pop under a unified branding strategy, and actively develop industry models connecting herbal wellness programmes with regional tourism.
